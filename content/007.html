Background

Background

Paint a new sprite, naming it Background.

Draw a circle, filled with a sandy color, then select it with the Reshape tool:

Drag the little blue circle at the bottom up slightly to make a rock shape:

Give the sandy fill colour a slight vertical gradient so that it's darker at the bottom (where it's in shade):

Zoom in and use the Reshape tool to make the shape look more natural (less symmetric):

Make a few of these rocks and let some of them overlap. Note: it's important that they all sit around the same height on the stage. If any rocks are too high they will appear to be floating in the sky. If any are too low it will spoil something called 'perspective'.

Foreground

Foreground

Paint a new sprite, naming it Foreground.

Draw some more rocks, again seating them around the same height on the stage as each other, but:

  • make them a little larger than the background rocks (because closer things appear larger),
  • position them all lower on the stage than the runner's feet (because closer things on the ground appear lower) and
  • make them a little darker than the background rocks (because colours tend to fade as they get further away).

Does your foreground look similar to the above?

Copy and paste the script from the Background into the Foreground. We just need to make a few tweaks.

Should this sprite go to the back layer?

It's the foreground so, yes, it should go to the front layer — make that tweak.

Should this sprite decrease its x position if its x position is greater than -437?

Type -999 in this sprite's x position, hit enter, and plug that number into the 'if x position greater than' slot.

Do closer objects appear to move faster than further away objects?

Look out of your side window next time you're riding in a car and you'll see that closer objects do indeed appear to move faster.

So, change x by -10 rather than -5.
